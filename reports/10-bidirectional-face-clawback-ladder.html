The Bidirectional Face, the Claw-Back Ladder, and the Shape of the Paper

Zhibai Jia · 7 July 2026 · the debt from #9 paid — the bidirectional certificate measured, the causal certificate rebuilt as a ladder, three clever predictions buried, and one invariant that keeps winning

What this note is. Note #9 measured the causal face because those checkpoints existed, and left two debts: the bidirectional face — our actual use case, where $\sigma_{\min}$/Faber is the proper certificate — and the certificates themselves, run on real Jacobians rather than a $5\times5$ toy. This note pays both, adds the three claw-back experiments that probe how much can be salvaged on the causal face, and reports the honest pattern the whole arc settled into. It closes with a loose sketch of the paper's logical spine, if we write it.
Result, in one paragraph. On the bidirectional band — near-normal $J$, measured $\nu$ inside Faber's domain — the screened-response certificate holds: edits decay two-sidedly, the measured reach sits an order of magnitude inside the envelope, and reach grows as $\sigma_{\min}$ falls. The certificate that headlines the paper is therefore measured on its proper face. But the envelope as we first computed it was a sophisticated heuristic, not a theorem (the Chebyshev rate applied to a non-normal matrix); a normality-free certified bound replaces it (measured reach sits $\sim\!100\times$ inside), so the claim now rests on a theorem — while the sharp field-of-values bound turns out to abstain on both faces ($0\in W(I{-}J)$ universally: a huge numerical range despite $\rho<1$), a fourth clever prediction dying on measurement. On the causal face the scalar certificate is vacuous, and we build a three-tier claw-back ladder (a-priori directional / a-posteriori residual / emergent metering) whose rungs degrade gracefully. And the arc's real lesson: three clever emergent-mechanism predictions died on contact with measurement (lazy evaluation, must-carry dissolution, a universal metering law) while the conditioning object kept over-predicting — which is the thesis the data chose for us.

1. The bidirectional face, measured — the debt from #9, paid

We retrained the equilibrium transformer with a two-sided window (each token attends to $[i-w,\,i+w]$) via a window curriculum, giving checkpoints bidir00–40 spanning $\sigma_{\min}(I-J)$ over $\sim\!15\times$. This is the face where the $\kappa\!\to\!\xi$ decay formula is the proper certificate: $J$ is banded and, crucially, near-normal — and we measure the departure rather than assume it.

2. The envelope was a heuristic. Now it is a theorem — but only the crude one survives.

A correction that matters for a rigor-forward venue. The reach formula $\xi=-1/\ln\frac{\sqrt\kappa-1}{\sqrt\kappa+1}$ is the Demko–Moss–Smith / Chebyshev rate, a theorem for Hermitian banded matrices. Our $(I-J)$ is near-normal ($\nu\approx0.3$), not Hermitian — and we even fed singular-value $\kappa$ into an eigenvalue-interval formula. As computed it is a sophisticated heuristic, and $\nu$ being smallish does not repair that. So we added two genuine bounds (c2_bidir.py, route_a_xi / route_b_xi) and ran them on the real Jacobians — which killed a prediction of our own and left a cleaner story:

Two findings, one good and one a self-inflicted null:

  • Route A certifies (the win). The normality-free bound is finite and loose (42–222 hops), and the measured reach sits $\sim\!100\times$ inside it. So “the envelope holds” is now a theorem — via the crude $\kappa$-route, no normality assumed. The ordering is certified-A $\gg$ proxy $\gg$ measured, with the proxy an excellent (uncertified) predictor of the true reach and Route A the sound-but-loose bound over it.
  • Route B abstains on both faces (the null). I predicted the sharp FOV bound would certify the bidirectional face and refuse only the causal one. Wrong: $+1\in W(J)$ everywhere — even at $\nu\approx0.3$. Verified directly: $\rho(J)\approx0.74$ but the numerical range reaches $\mathrm{Re}\,W(J)\approx3.1$ ($\lVert J\rVert\approx5$–$6.5$, numerical radius $w(J)/\rho(J)\approx4\times$). The trained equilibrium transformer has a huge field of values despite a small spectral radius, so the singularity at $+1$ is swallowed and the sharp certificate is defeated by non-normality on both faces.
The failure mode is not a causal-only edge case — and it corrects Note #9. Route B abstains exactly when $0\in W(I-J)$, which holds while $J$ is invertible ($\sigma_{\min}>0$) — and here it holds universally. So Note #9's “Faber is the proper certificate on the bidirectional face” was over-stated. What is proper there is that $\sigma_{\min}$/conditioning governs reach (measured; certified via Route A); the specific Faber-on-FOV rate is not certifiable in this model class. A referee's first question — “did you check $0\notin W(I-J)$?” — now has an honest answer: we did, it fails, we fall back to the sound $\kappa$ bound. $\nu\approx0.3$ is “near-normal” only in the relative Frobenius sense; it is nowhere near enough to pull $W$ off the singularity. This is a fourth clever prediction dying on measurement (§4) — and again the crude conditioning object is what survives.

3. The causal face is vacuous — so we build a ladder

On the causal face the scalar $\xi$-ball is vacuous: $\sigma_{\min}$ reports the carry direction and charges every edit as if its whole perturbation lay there. Every per-hop transfer norm exceeds $1$ (up to $25\times$), so the scalar product bound predicts growth while the directional product decays. The response is a ladder of three certificates of decreasing guarantee strength, each matched to what survives:

The interface with causality is the entire point: the a-priori spatial certificate behaves oppositely on the two faces, dictated by $J$'s geometry. Non-normality collapses the scalar bound $\Rightarrow$ Tier 1 recovers locality directionally, Tier 2 is the unconditional residual backstop, and Tier 3 prices what it can. The bidirectional face barely needs the ladder; the causal face is made of it.

Tier 1 validated (C2d). Re-blocking the causal band at window granularity makes it block-bidiagonal (“one hop = one window” as a theorem, not a convention). The directional product decays even as the scalar norm-product bound's slack explodes $7.5\times\!\to\!764\times$ over hops 2–4 (every per-hop norm $>1$). At trained conditioning, direction is not a refinement — it is the entire content of the causal certificate. The rank-deficient / carry-subspace math is underwritten by the singular-value pseudoinverse-decay theorem (Shin–Tan–Anitescu 2411.04400), which is also what makes Route B's transverse-complement decay rigorous — the two claw-backs are the same theorem on the two faces.

4. Three clever predictions died. One dumb object kept winning.

This is the part worth being honest about, because it reshaped the paper's center of gravity. Across the arc we kept proposing emergent mechanisms — the solver learning to be clever — and they kept failing, while the plain conditioning object kept predicting more than we asked of it.

Death 1 — lazy evaluation (C2t, NEGATIVE). Prediction: a bidirectional relay would defer the cost of an edit whose reader hasn't arrived. It doesn't. Write-cost of editing an unqueried value is reader-independent on all three substrates (causal $7.6\!\approx\!7.8$, read-only $12.9\!\approx\!13.0$, query-visible $15.3\!\approx\!15.7$ iters). Must-carry is empirically robust, and selective forgetting did not emerge even where architecturally permitted — nothing in the recall loss rewards it. (Path-independence held to $10^{-7}$: a clean warm-start-exactness sub-result fell out of the null.)
Death 2 — must-carry dissolution. We predicted a query-aware bidirectional relay would drop the carry of task-irrelevant bindings. It doesn't: irrelevant-edit far/near is $0.061$ (bidir40) vs $0.068$ (curr40) — essentially identical. The reader-set principle explains why (below): invisible readers force carry in any architecture. The earlier “dissolves” contrast was an artefact of comparing against a stale $\xi\!\approx\!27$ baseline.
Death 3 — a universal metering law (C2m, PARTIAL). We predicted a face-agnostic “cost meters output movement” law. It is clean only on the bidirectional face (Spearman 0.90/0.92/0.89, input norm adds nothing). On the causal face it is weak and mode-confounded (0.67/0.65 with negative partials $-0.49/-0.66$: carry-aligned movement rides slow modes, so equal movement costs unequal iterations) and absent at near-singularity (0.09). Metering is a bidirectional property; the causal face gets only a coarse step. This inverted the ladder's tier-3 assignment.
The survivor. The pre-solve forecast $\lVert R\,\delta h\rVert$ (with $R=(I-J)^{-1}$) predicts realized $\lVert\Delta z\rVert$ at Spearman 0.96–1.00 on both faces at every conditioning. Every death was a mechanism we hoped the network had learned; the one thing that worked everywhere was reading it off the resolvent. $\sigma_{\min}$, $\nu$, and $R$ — the conditioning of the linearized fixed point — are the objects that carry the paper. The clever stories are, at best, measured mechanism inside a sound conditioning envelope.

5. The honest boundary: edit-locality is dual to forgetting

The one thing a certificate-of-locality paper must not hide: on the causal face, an edit being local is the same event as the model having forgotten. The screening length $\xi$ that bounds edit-reach is the memory horizon. A causal language model doing its job — carrying a fact to a distant cursor — therefore has non-local edits by necessity, and “cheap local maintenance” is available exactly when the edit didn't matter downstream. The scalar $\xi$-ball on the causal face is sound but vacuous for any edit that a reader will consume.

The reader-set principle states the general law: selectivity is possible exactly with respect to readers attendable at solve time; unknown/future readers force carry in any architecture. Causal attention is the special case where all readers are structurally future. So the genuine win is not “cheap causal maintenance” — it is the bidirectional local-readout niche: encoder-style edits where the readers sit in the solved context (certified partial KV-cache invalidation, incremental re-embedding), which is precisely where the Faber face and its now-certified envelope apply. We should scope the paper to that niche and say so plainly.

6. The shape of the paper (a loose sketch, if we write it)

The arc has stopped moving, so the spine is visible. Provisional title: Conditioning, Not Contraction: Certifying Local Edits in Deep Equilibrium Transformers. Six beats:

  1. One invariant. The maintainability of an equilibrium transformer's fixed point under a mid-context edit is governed by $\sigma_{\min}(I-J)$ — the conditioning of the linearized map — not by contraction $\rho$. $\rho$ is neither necessary nor sufficient (local at $\rho=8.4$; the graph resolvent's $1-\rho$ and the linear/SSM corner are special cases).
  2. The operation only exists at equilibrium. The maintenance move — edit, then warm-start local re-solve — needs $(I-J)^{-1}$, which exists only because there is a fixed point to perturb. Feedforward truncation diverges in the expressive $\rho>1$ regime; a fused SSM state has no mid-context edit operation. Equilibrium is load-bearing, not incidental.
  3. One structural fact splits the certificate into two faces. Jacobian geometry, with $\nu$ (departure from normality) as the measured discriminator that orders the faces: bidirectional / less-non-normal $\Rightarrow$ a screened elliptic response whose reach is certified by the normality-free $\sigma_{\min}$/$\kappa$ bound (the sharp Faber-on-FOV rate is defeated by the model's large numerical range, $0\in W(I{-}J)$ — §2); causal / strongly-non-normal $\Rightarrow$ a forward relay certified by a product–Lyapunov form, with the scalar version vacuous.
  4. A certificate ladder, rigor stated per rung. A-priori directional (Tier 1) / a-posteriori residual (Tier 2, unconditional) / emergent metering (Tier 3, measured, not claimed). The causal claw-back and the bidirectional carry decomposition are one theorem (pseudoinverse-on-the-transverse-complement) deployed on both faces.
  5. An honest scope. Edit-locality is dual to forgetting; causal maintenance is self-defeating for the edits that matter. The contribution lives in the bidirectional local-readout niche (certified partial cache invalidation), and the reader-set principle says exactly why. We state this as a boundary, not bury it.
  6. The thesis the data chose. Conditioning predicts reach, cost, and error across both faces (the resolvent forecast, Spearman $\ge0.96$); the clever emergent-mechanism hypotheses did not survive measurement. The paper is about the invariant, not the stories — a translation placing the numerical-analysis and RNN-dynamics decay lenses onto the equilibrium-transformer maintenance object, with the mechanisms reported as measured behaviour inside a certified envelope.
Positioning, in one line. Not new decay mathematics (Vogt / Cirone are narrow anchors; Benzi–Golub and Shin–Tan–Anitescu are the load-bearing backbones we cite) — the unoccupied sliver is reading the resolvent as a spatial edit-reach certificate on a nonlinear equilibrium attention model, the IVP/BVP regime split, and the warm-start maintenance operation with a $\sigma_{\min}$ uniqueness boundary. Venue: TMLR (rigor-forward fit).

7. What is owed before drafting

  • Certified-bound numbers — done (§2). Route A certifies (42–222 hops, measured $\sim\!100\times$ inside); Route B abstains universally ($0\in W(I{-}J)$). A tighter certified rate would need a region sharper than the field of values (e.g. pseudospectral $\varepsilon$-tuning) — open, but not needed for soundness.
  • C2i — edit interference. Paired edits vs sum-of-singles vs separation: the linear-regime boundary (superposition until attention patterns re-form). Owed, not yet run.
  • Insert/delete. The aligned-frame reduction (a width-$w$ substitution at the cut) on the pure-relative bidirnp substrate. Owed, not yet run.
  • Reading, not running. The four backbone papers are read (see sequence_certificate_literature.md, full-read corrections); the remaining work before text is the draft itself.
  • Slated, not this paper. The $\nu$ semi-causal dial (C2$\nu$: asymmetric band, to disentangle “$\nu$ governs” from “causality is special”), the saturated-attention linearity mechanism behind the forecast's accuracy, and conditioned-collage training. Multistability is explicitly out of scope.
