What the Equilibrium Buys: A Maintainability Framework for Editable Graph Models, and an Honest Expressivity Null

Zhibai Jia · 29 June 2026 · consolidates the conversion-principle, the maintenance taxonomy, and the head-to-head

What this note is. Note #7 established that local maintainability is governed by $\sigma_{\min}(I-J)$, not contraction. This note asks the harder follow-up that #7 deferred: given that, what does an equilibrium model actually buy over the alternatives, and over which baseline class? The answer reshaped the thesis. We report it straight, including a null result on expressivity that we went looking to confirm rather than avoid.
Thesis, after the dust settled. The contribution is not "more expressive than other editable models." It is a maintainability framework: a single condition $\sigma_{\min}(I-J)>0$ characterizes edit-locality for the entire equilibrium family — linear PPR (InstantGNN), nonlinear, signed, interleaved — and a single algorithm (warm-start local re-solve) maintains all of them, including in the non-contractive ($\rho(J)>1$) regime and across semirings (sum-product and tropical max-plus). Expressivity is a per-task knob within that family — on a fixed semiring it ties the strongest maintainable incumbent; the one real, controlled difference is the semiring itself (§5), which the linear-push incumbents cannot leave. The science is the characterization, not a horse-race win.

1. The right reference class: editable models, split by maintenance mechanism

"More expressive than every GNN on local tasks" was never the claim and is hopeless: for a reach-$k$ local target a $k$-layer network is expressively sufficient (we verify this in §4). The claim that matters is among models that admit cheap local edit-maintenance. That class splits cleanly by how an edit propagates — and the split is the whole story.

classformmaintain after an editexpressivity

The unifying observation: a weight-tied interleaved net run to convergence is the equilibrium; finite-depth is a truncated equilibrium; decoupled-linear is the linear resolvent (with the bonus of exact delta-push). The same resolvent-decay theory and the single knob $\sigma_{\min}(I-J)$ explain the edit-locality of all three. The decoupled class is editable because linear superposition lets you push a delta; interleaving nonlinearity destroys that, so the expressive rows are maintainable only by local re-solve — which is exactly what the equilibrium framing makes into a single clean operation instead of $L$-layer bookkeeping.

2. A controlled testbed: a local operator teacher

To probe expressivity on a local graph we built a random teacher whose label is the squared energy of a local operator applied to random node features $X$, on a 2-D grid: $$ s_i \;=\; \sum_{r} a_r\,\big((\mathcal{O}^{(k)}\,X w_r)_i\big)^2, \qquad \mathcal{O}\in\{\,L=I-\hat A\ (\text{Laplacian}),\ \text{neighbour-mean},\ \text{directional}\,\}. $$ Order $k$ sets the reach ($k{=}1$ Dirichlet, $k{=}2$ biharmonic $\Delta^2$), giving a principled locality knob — and a pleasing mirror of the theory: the task is a low-degree polynomial in $L$, while maintenance is a low-degree polynomial in $J$. Two variants matter: propagate-then-square $(L^k\psi)^2$ and square-then-propagate $\overline{(\psi^2)}$; a feature-only MLP is near-chance on the leak-free variants (the label lives in the neighbourhood, not the node).

3. The maintenance result (clean)

Train the general per-edge-nonlinear MPNN as an equilibrium on the biharmonic task, delete an interior node, and re-solve by warm-starting from the old fixed point. Measured against the model-editing triple (reliability, generality, locality) plus cost:

propertymeasurementresult
Reliability / exactnesswarm-start vs cold (from-zero) re-solve identical to $4.7\times10^{-7}$ (path-independent — warm-start is free correctness)
Localityedit-response decay vs graph distance clean exponential, screening length $\xi\approx 1.1$ hops
Costwarm vs cold iterations; prediction ring radius $40$ vs $61$ iters ($1.5\times$); predictions move only within $6$ hops $\ll$ diameter $78$
The headline diagnostic. This model had $\rho(J)=0.955$ — a hair under the contraction boundary — yet $\xi\approx 1$ hop. A model can be barely contractive and still sharply edit-local, because what governs the decay is the distance of the spectrum from $+1$ ($\sigma_{\min}(I-J)$), not $\rho$. This is the §7 thesis made concrete on a real trained model.

On a high-diameter graph the dominant cost lever is not the $1.5\times$ warm-start but the ring radius: only a $6$-hop ball ($\sim$113 of 1600 nodes) actually moves, so one re-solves a small local patch and freezes the rest. This is the non-contractive generalization of InstantGNN's linear incremental update — valid because $(I-J)$ is well-conditioned, not because it is a contraction.

3b. Is $\sigma_{\min}(I-J)$ a law for edit-locality, or folklore? (the $\xi$ sweep)

The §3 callout asserts the conditioning of $(I-J)$ — not $\rho(J)$ — governs the screening length. We tested it quantitatively. Sweep the contraction knob $s_{\max}$ (with $\texttt{jac\_gamma}=0$, so $s_{\max}$ is the only lever) across $22$ trained cells (Anderson-solved so the fixed point is reached even near the boundary). At each landed fixed point form the dense Jacobian, read the $2$-norm condition number $\kappa_2(I-J)=\sigma_{\max}/\sigma_{\min}$, convert to the Demko–Moss–Smith / Faber decay rate $q=\tfrac{\sqrt{\kappa}-1}{\sqrt{\kappa}+1}$, and compare the predicted reach $\xi_{\text{pred}}=-1/\ln q$ to the measured reach $\xi_{\text{meas}}$ (log-slope of the edit response vs graph distance after a node deletion). Three findings, two of them caveats — reported in full:

claimmeasurementresult
Faber/$\sigma_{\min}$ reach is a valid upper bound $\xi_{\text{meas}}<\xi_{\text{pred}}$ across the sweep holds on $22/22$ configs (ratio $0.20$–$0.81$)
Tight (proportional) in the edit-local binding regime $\kappa\le 8$: fit $\xi_{\text{meas}}\approx a\,\xi_{\text{pred}}$ $a\approx 0.6$, Pearson $r\approx 0.87$–$0.92$, $R^2\approx 0.5$–$0.7$
Loose near singularity ($\sigma_{\min}\to 0$) $\kappa>40$: $\xi_{\text{pred}}$ vs $\xi_{\text{meas}}$ $\xi_{\text{pred}}\!=\!3.4$–$4.0$ but $\xi_{\text{meas}}\!\approx\!0.7$–$0.9$ — worst-case $\ne$ typical reach
The law, stated honestly. The conditioning of $(I-J)$ is a rigorous upper bound on edit-reach everywhere, and a tight, proportional predictor in the regime where the bound binds (small $\kappa$, $\xi\lesssim 1.5$ hops — exactly the edit-local regime we care about). So “editability is governed by $\sigma_{\min}(I-J)$” is a quantitative, falsifiable statement, not folklore. Near singularity the bound goes conservative: the worst-case mode that $\sigma_{\min}$ tracks is not the typical perturbation, so real operators stay more local than the bound — the “local in name only” failure mode (§7) is a worst case the trained models did not hit.

What this sweep does not show. Because $s_{\max}$ moves $\rho(J)$ and $\sigma_{\min}(I-J)$ together — and the trained cell self-limits to $\rho\le 0.97$ throughout, never crossing the contraction boundary — the two scalars are collinear here and predict $\xi_{\text{meas}}$ about equally well (Spearman $\approx 0.81$ vs $0.84$). This sweep therefore confirms the magnitude law but cannot by itself crown $\sigma_{\min}$ over $\rho$. The dissociation — $\rho(J)>1$ yet still edit-local, where the $\rho$-based Neumann intuition predicts divergence and only $\sigma_{\min}$/dist$(1,\,\mathrm{spec})$ survives — rests on the separate $\rho\approx1.3$ runs (fagcn_deq_locality roman, broyden_conditioning), not on this one. (sigma_min_law.py.)

4. The expressivity null (honest)

We went looking to confirm that the nonlinear equilibrium out-expresses the maintainable incumbents on local tasks. It does not, robustly. Three experiments.

4a. Going to equilibrium buys no local expressivity over a matched unroll

Our own cell, unrolled $K\approx\text{reach}$ steps (a weight-tied finite-depth net), matches the full equilibrium — because reach $=$ edit-length $=\xi$, so an edit-local equilibrium is just a depth-$\sim\xi$ net.

4b. Against the linear maintainable incumbent: a wash

InstantGNN's propagation is a linear fixed point $z=\alpha h_0+(1-\alpha)\hat A z$. Build it with the same encoder, the same ego-separated head, the same solver — only the message is linear. Both are edit-local ($\xi<1$ hop) and exactly maintainable (warm $=$ cold). Expressivity:

taskInstantGNN-linear (PPR)ours (MPNN-DEQ)
Laplacian (propagate-then-square)0.4580.543
interleaved (prop→relu→prop→square)0.5420.508

$\pm 0.05$, task-dependent — the linear incumbent even wins the interleaved task. The reason is instructive: a full-resolvent linear propagation plus a nonlinear head already covers propagate-then-square (the head does the square), and relu has a dominant linear component, so an relu-interleaved target is mostly decoupled-expressible. A target that truly needs interleaving (a pure-square interior nonlinearity, $(L(L\psi)^2)^2$) would separate them — but it also requires a pre-aggregation square, which our own cell cannot learn well (it scores 0.155 on the square-then-propagate task). The separating tasks are exactly the ones nobody learns.

Null result, stated plainly. On controlled local tasks, the nonlinear equilibrium does not reliably out-express the strongest maintainable incumbent (a linear equilibrium with a nonlinear head). Where one model wins it is by $\le 0.09$ and the direction flips with the task. §5 explains why — we were testing inside the wrong algebra — and finds the one axis where a real, controlled difference does appear.

4c. Real heterophily does not rescue it

The obvious retort is "use real data." On roman-empire the signed nonlinear equilibrium beats linear propagation by $+27$ points (SGC $0.35\to$ ours $0.68$) — but it clears the graph-free MLP ($0.644$) by only $+3.8$. So the $+27$ is "linear low-pass is actively harmful on heterophily," not "our propagation extracts real graph signal beyond features." With the MLP that close, propagation's contribution is suspicious, and roman is not clean evidence of an expressivity win either.

5. The semiring axis: where a real difference does appear

The null in §4 has a precise cause. Manifold/spectral operators are functions of the Laplacian, $g(L)$ — and $g(L)X$ is, for any filter $g$ (high-pass or low-pass), a linear functional of $X$. So the task $(L^kX)^2$ is $\varphi(\text{linear aggregate})$, which is exactly what a linear-propagation model with a nonlinear head computes. Linear algebra is the sum-product semiring ($\sum_j L_{ij}f_j$); we had been building tasks inside the incumbent's own algebra, so a tie was inevitable.

The escape is an order statistic — $\max$ over the neighbourhood — which is not a function of any linear aggregate. That is the tropical (max-plus) semiring $(\max,+)$: Viterbi, Bellman–Ford, DTW. The neural-algorithmic-reasoning line establishes that the aggregator must match the algorithm's semiring (Veličković Neural Execution, 2019; Xu et al. algorithmic alignment, 2020; Dudzik–Veličković GNNs are Dynamic Programmers, 2022). We test it directly with a $2\times2$: a spectral task $(L^2\psi)^2$ vs a tropical task (the $\gamma$-discounted max-reachable feature $z_i=\max(\psi_i,\gamma\max_{j}z_j)$), each against sum-, max-, and learnable-temperature log-sum-exp aggregation (40×40 grid, test $R^2$).

model \ taskspectral (linear)tropical (max-plus)
InstantGNN-linear (sum)0.4590.481
MPNN sum-agg0.5670.529
MPNN max-agg0.5530.683
MPNN log-sum-exp (learn $\beta$)0.5990.497

The controlled comparison is the diagnostic one: changing only the aggregator from sum to max inside the same cell lifts the tropical score $0.566\to0.683$ ($+0.12$, $\sim\!4\sigma$) while costing nothing on spectral. So there is a real, mechanistic expressivity difference — it is the aggregation algebra, and the linear-push incumbents are locked in the sum row (max-plus has no linear push).

Why the gap is $+0.12$ and not categorical — a locality bound. A sum-model does not collapse to zero on the tropical task ($0.48$–$0.57$) because an edit-local neighbourhood is low-dimensional: a wide linear-propagation readout takes $\sim\!d$ linear measurements of it, and if $d\gtrsim$ (neighbourhood size) an MLP head can approximately recover the individual neighbours and compute anything — including $\max$. So on strictly-local tasks a wide linear-readout model is approximately universal, and expressivity gaps are inherently bounded (they grow only with degree or range, which trade against locality). The expressivity tie of §4 is, near enough, a consequence of demanding edit-locality — not a failure of task design.

A single model that spans semirings — as a generalist, not a specialist. The natural learner is one log-sum-exp aggregator with a learnable temperature ($\beta\to0$: mean; $\beta\to\infty$: max). Out of the box it under-sharpens ($\beta$ stuck $\approx1.3$, tropical only $0.497$) — but that is a fixable artifact: weight decay pulls $\beta$ down and it shares the small base learning rate. Freeing $\beta$ (own param group, no weight decay, higher lr, high init) recovers most of it: one model reaches spectral $0.580$ ($\beta\!\approx\!4.9$) and tropical $0.593$ ($\beta\!\approx\!7.9$) — $\beta$ moves toward max where the task is tropical, and the tropical score jumps $0.497\to0.593$. So a single smooth model genuinely covers both columns. It still trails the hard-max specialist by $\sim\!0.09$ on the pure max-plus target (a finite-$\beta$ soft-max averages the top few neighbours), so hard max remains the sharpest tropical demonstrator — but "one model spans semirings" is now supported, with a soft-vs-hard gap rather than a failure.

And the tropical equilibrium stays maintainable — under the same characterization. The max-agg cell, trained on the tropical task and probed by node deletion, is exactly maintainable (warm $=$ cold to $4.7\times10^{-7}$) and sharply local ($\xi\approx0.6$ hop). On a small grid where the dense Jacobian is formable, $\sigma_{\min}(I-J)=0.28$ and $\mathrm{dist}(1,\mathrm{spec})=0.34$ are healthy and predict $\xi$; the only tropical-specific subtlety is that $\max$ is non-smooth, but the argmax routing switches only in the edit core ($54\%$ within 2 hops, $\sim\!1\%$ beyond 3) — the far field, which is the locality, has a frozen routing, so the Chebyshev–Faber argument behind $\sigma_{\min}$ applies cleanly. (A log-sum-exp cell, being smooth, removes even that subtlety.) So one condition, $\sigma_{\min}(I-J)$, governs edit-locality across both semirings. [Correction to an earlier draft: a $\rho(J)=1.4$ figure for the tropical cell was a power-method artifact on the non-smooth subgradient; the dense value is $\rho=0.66<1$, and it is not a sup-norm contraction.]

6. So what is the contribution?

Strip away what did not survive and three things remain, all supported by the experiments above:

  1. A characterization. $\sigma_{\min}(I-J)$ — the distance of the spectrum from $+1$, not $\rho$ — governs edit-locality of an implicit model. This is non-obvious: a fixed point looks global yet is provably $\xi$-local. It is trivial for finite-depth and is the content for equilibria. (Note #7 theory; the $\rho{=}0.955,\ \xi{\approx}1$ datum in §3.)
  2. An algorithm. Warm-start local re-solve maintains nonlinear / signed / interleaved equilibria after an edit — the generalization of InstantGNN's linear incremental update to the regime where no delta-push exists, including non-contractive $\rho(J)>1$ fixed points found by Broyden.
  3. A unification, across semirings. The same condition $\sigma_{\min}(I-J)$ and the same warm-start algorithm cover the whole family — decoupled-linear (InstantGNN, the linear-resolvent special case), nonlinear sum-product, and tropical max-plus (§5). Expressivity is a knob you turn within a uniformly-maintainable family, and the linear-push incumbents can only ever sit in the sum-product corner of it.

7. Limitations & open checks

  • The expressivity advantage is real but bounded: it appears on the semiring axis (sum→max, $+0.12$ controlled, §5), not on any single-nonlinearity task within a fixed semiring, and locality caps its magnitude. We do not claim a broad "more expressive" result.
  • "One model spans semirings" (learnable-temperature log-sum-exp) works as a generalist once $\beta$ is freed from weight decay (spectral $0.580$, tropical $0.593$, $\beta$ dialling $4.9\!\to\!7.9$), but a soft aggregator still trails the hard-max specialist by $\sim\!0.09$ on the pure max-plus target. The clean single-model dial is a soft-vs-hard compromise, not a free lunch.
  • The tropical maintenance leans on a frozen-routing linearisation; it holds because routing switches are core-confined ($\sim\!1\%$ far-field), but a fully nonlinear (max-plus / Thompson-metric) treatment is the rigorous version we have not written.
  • The warm-start speedup ($1.5\times$–$3\times$ in iterations) is modest; the real lever is ring-truncation on high-diameter graphs — still to be shown at scale on a large real graph.
  • Novelty of the bridge is still pending: is "InstantGNN generalized to nonlinear equilibria $+$ a $\sigma_{\min}$ edit-locality characterization" genuinely open, or folklore? (See the editability literature record; an advisor gut-check is queued.)
