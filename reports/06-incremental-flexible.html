Beyond Linear Propagation: Contraction as the Door to Flexible Incremental GNNs

Zhibai Jia · 23 June 2026 · early-stage direction note, results preliminary

Status. This is a direction note, not a finished study. It records a reframing (why the earlier "unlearning" angle is better cast as incremental maintenance), the gap we think is open, and a single proof-of-life experiment (a recurrentized FAGCN that solves and trains). The accuracy bake-off and the maintenance transfer are future work.

1. The pivot: this was never unlearning

Notes #5 framed re-solving an equilibrium after a graph edit as "exact unlearning." On reflection that is the wrong word. Learning updates parameters from data; unlearning scrubs a training point's influence from those parameters. What we actually do leaves the weights frozen and recomputes the contextualized embeddings after the graph changes — a "circle-and-recompute." Its natural home is not privacy but maintenance of large production GNNs: million-node recommenders where nodes and edges churn constantly, where retraining is prohibitive on any short timescale, and where the enriched node embeddings feed many downstream tasks. Keeping those embeddings fresh and locally-correct after each edit is the real problem. (Privacy survives only as a thin corollary: an attacker holding stale latents but not the weights is thwarted by a re-solve.)

2. The incumbent paradigm edits linear propagation

That problem already has a field: incremental / dynamic-inference GNNs — InstantGNN (KDD 2022), DynAnom, IDOL, RIPPLE/RIPPLE++, Layph. They are strikingly uniform. Almost all are decoupled: a parameter-free linear propagation (Personalized / generalized PageRank, forward-push) followed by a per-node MLP head. On a graph edit they exploit the algebraic invariants of that linear propagation to adjust only the affected nodes, with error provably equal to recomputation.

The structural reason — and the bottleneck. Their cheap exact updates exist because propagation is linear: push/PPR invariants are a property of linear operators. That is also the ceiling. Linear propagation is a fixed low-pass filter, which assumes homophily; it cannot represent the feature-dependent, signed, or attention-based interactions that the rest of deep learning relies on. The incumbent paradigm bought maintainability by giving up the DL toolbox.

3. Why linear propagation is a low-pass trap

With symmetric-normalized adjacency $\hat{A}=D^{-1/2}AD^{-1/2}$ and Laplacian $L=I-\hat{A}=U\Lambda U^{\top}$ ($\lambda\in[0,2]$), a graph convolution is a spectral filter $g(L)=U\,g(\Lambda)\,U^{\top}$. Small $\lambda$ are smooth components; large $\lambda$ are sign-flipping (high-frequency) components. Standard propagation $\hat{A}=I-L$ is

$$g_{\text{GCN}}(\lambda) = 1-\lambda,$$

a low-pass filter: it keeps smooth signal and discards high frequency. That is right for homophily (the label signal is smooth) and destructive for heterophily, where neighbors differ and the discriminative signal is high-frequency. SGC, APPNP, and the PPR propagation inside InstantGNN are all in this low-pass family.

4. The claim: contraction, not linearity, is the real enabler

The maintainability the incumbents get from linearity, an equilibrium model gets from contraction — a strictly larger design space. If a fixed-point map is a contraction, its solution is unique and a local edit re-converges locally (the influence of a perturbation decays with graph distance). Linearity-with-bounded-spectral-radius is just one sufficient way to be a contraction; nonlinear, signed, or attention-based maps qualify too, as long as they stay contractive. So the maintenance property is available to the full DL toolbox, not only to linear propagation.

Concretely, take a contractive equilibrium with input injection $Ax$ and a (possibly signed, state-dependent) propagation operator $M$:

$$z \;=\; \varepsilon z + Ax + sMz \;\;\Longrightarrow\;\; z^{\star}=\big((1-\varepsilon)I - sM\big)^{-1}Ax.$$

When $\varepsilon + s\,\rho(M) < 1$ this fixed point is unique and equals a convergent series in $M$ — an infinite-order graph filter. The same inequality does double duty:

One inequality, two payoffs. $\varepsilon + s\,\rho(M)<1$ simultaneously (i) bounds the filter so the equilibrium exists and is unique — and if $M$ carries signed weights, this is a frequency-adaptive filter with both low- and high-pass response (heterophily-capable); and (ii) makes $\lVert (sM)^k\rVert$ decay geometrically, so a node deletion is a local perturbation re-solvable by warm-start + ring truncation (the maintenance property). Expressiveness and maintainability are governed by the same knob.

5. Proof of life: recurrentized FAGCN

To make the signed/heterophily case concrete we recurrentize FAGCN (Bo et al. 2021), whose signed attention is the canonical "beyond low-frequency" mechanism, into a state-dependent contractive equilibrium:

$$z_i' \;=\; \varepsilon\, z_i \;+\; (Ax)_i \;+\; \sum_{j\in\mathcal{N}(i)} \frac{\alpha_{ij}}{\sqrt{d_i d_j}}\, z_j, \qquad \alpha_{ij}=\tanh\!\big(g^{\top}[z_i\,\Vert\,z_j]\big)\in[-1,1].$$

The $\tanh$ makes the attention signed (so it can subtract dissimilar neighbors — high-pass), $\alpha$ is computed from the evolving $z$ (genuinely nonlinear depth), and $Ax$ is the per-step input injection a fixed point requires. We solve it with TorchDEQ (fixed_point_iter + PhantomGrad). Contraction is not guaranteed a priori for state-dependent signed attention — this is exactly the regime where we have seen multistability before — so we treat $(\varepsilon, s)$ as the control and verify convergence.

6. Where this sits, honestly

We are candid that none of the ingredients is new in isolation. The maintenance neighbourhood is crowded: InstantGNN already does local incremental updates with recompute-error guarantees (for the linear case). Re-solving a cut-out region is mechanically regeneration, as in Neural Cellular Automata (Mordvintsev et al. 2020; Graph-NCA), and the NCA↔DEQ equivalence is our own prior note (arXiv:2501.03573). FAGCN is an existing architecture. What we are attempting to assemble from these is a single, currently-unoccupied claim:

The bet. Contraction generalizes the incremental-GNN trick from linear propagation to the full nonlinear / attention / (eventually multimodal) toolbox. You can take a strong architecture — here FAGCN — recurrentize it into a contractive equilibrium, and inherit both its expressiveness (which InstantGNN lacks) and local incremental maintainability (which feedforward FAGCN lacks). Linearity is a corner of the contraction space, not its boundary.

7. What has to happen next

  1. Regularize the recurrentized FAGCN (dropout, capacity, early-stop) to a fair heterophily accuracy.
  2. Well-posedness gate — uniqueness (fixed-point gap across random inits), not just convergence; and an $(\varepsilon, s)$ sweep to map where expressiveness breaks contraction into multistability.
  3. Accuracy head-to-head vs MLP / SGC / APPNP / feedforward FAGCN on filtered Chameleon, Squirrel, then the larger high-diameter roman-empire.
  4. Maintenance transfer — the payoff: show the warm-start + ring-truncation deletion mechanics from Note #5 carry over to this expressive, signed model, which the linear incumbents cannot represent and the feedforward original cannot maintain.
