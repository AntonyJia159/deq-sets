Structured Edits: Insert/Delete as Operator Bordering

Zhibai Jia · 15 July 2026 · a value edit is a vector the resolvent eats; an insert changes the operator's size — the native language is matrix bordering + Schur complement. What the tiers inherit, how far the shadow reaches (measured), and why the cheap adjoint is not rigorous.

Scope. Note #11 covers value (fixed-size) edits: $\delta z=(I-J)^{-1}\delta h$, the reach envelope, the residual and reader-set certificates. This note is the structural companion — insert, delete, splice — which change the operator's dimension and so need a different piece of linear algebra. Three registers, kept apart: the algebra (bordering/Schur, exact), the measured shadow decay and rank, and one measured negative (no cheap rigorous adjoint). Everything is toy-scale; the warm-start / RAG payoffs are proposals pending C7.

1. Why a structural edit is different

A value edit changes the injection in a fixed space, $h\to h+\delta h$; the operator $M=(I-J)$ keeps its shape and the resolvent simply eats the perturbation, $\delta z^\star=M^{-1}\delta h$ (Note #11). An insert or delete changes the sequence length, so $M$ changes size and every downstream index shifts. Under an absolute positional embedding that re-indexing is itself a dense edit: even a semantically null filler insert changes every shifted token's $h_0$, casting a shadow over the whole downstream. Under a relative substrate (NO_POSW) a global shift is invisible to attention except at the cut — so insert/delete reduces to a width-$w$ content substitution straddling the cut. This is the aligned-frame reduction, and §4 measures that it is real. Everything algebraic below lives in that aligned frame.

The one-line lesson, one level up. Locality of a structural edit is genuine only in coordinates attached to content (token-attached blocks). Index-attached blocking manufactures a fake "all-blocks-changed." It is the relative-vs-absolute-PE lesson again: structural edits are local in every structure attached to content, global in every structure attached to coordinates.

2. The bordering algebra

Insert a token at cut $c$. In the aligned frame the new operator $M'$ is the old $M$ bordered by the new token's couplings — a new row/column plus a self-block:

$$ M' = \begin{pmatrix} M & b \\ c^{\top} & \delta \end{pmatrix}, \qquad b=\text{(old}\to\text{new)},\; c^{\top}=\text{(new}\to\text{old)},\; \delta=\text{(self)}. $$

Windowed attention makes $b,c$ nonzero only for old blocks inside the new token's window — they are spatially supported at the cut. That locality is the whole engine. Eliminating the new token (block Gaussian elimination) gives the old-block correction through the Schur complement $S=\delta-c^{\top}M^{-1}b$ (a $d\times d$ effective stiffness):

$$ [M'^{-1}]_{\text{old,old}} = M^{-1} + (M^{-1}b)\,S^{-1}\,(c^{\top}M^{-1}), \qquad \det M' = \det M \cdot \det S. $$

So the inserted token perturbs the cached resolvent $R=M^{-1}$ by a rank-$\le d$ update sourced at the cut, and all of the new conditioning risk is carried by one local object: $\sigma_{\min}(S)$, the new-token stiffness, computable from $R$-blocks near the cut. Delete is the exact dual (downdating): remove token $b$ and the remaining inverse is $R_{AA}-R_{Ab}(R_{bb})^{-1}R_{bA}$ — subtract every resolvent path routed through the deleted token, gated by $\|R_{bb}^{-1}\|$ = the token's diagonal resolvent block, i.e. its hub-ness.

Theorem (a): the per-edit two-term envelope — spatial

Apply the cached tier-1 reach bound to each leg of the correction ($R$ acting on a cut-local source decays at the old rate, $\|[M^{-1}b]_i\|\le C'\rho^{d(i,\text{cut})}$, likewise for $c^{\top}M^{-1}$). The new response reach is

$$ \big\|[R_{\text{new}}]_{ij}\big\| \;\le\; \underbrace{C\,\rho^{\,d(i,j)}}_{\text{direct}} \;+\; \underbrace{\frac{C'C''}{\sigma_{\min}(S)}\,\rho^{\,d(i,\text{cut})+d(\text{cut},j)}}_{\text{through the new token}}. $$

Physically the inserted token is a scattering center at the cut: any $i\to j$ response gains one extra path (in to the cut, scatter with gain $1/\sigma_{\min}(S)$, out to $j$), each leg decaying at the old rate $\rho$. Since $d(i,\text{cut})+d(\text{cut},j)\ge d(i,j)$ (triangle inequality), the correction is never slower-decaying than the direct term — a subdominant bump localized at the cut. Rate inherited, constant picks up one factor. Nothing geometric is recomputed ($C,\rho,R$-blocks cached); the only new number is the scalar gate $\sigma_{\min}(S)$, from local blocks. This is what "the certificates close under structural edits" means concretely.

Theorem (b): the cumulative Stein budget — temporal

A different question: not "does this edit's envelope hold" but "how many edits before the cached geometry certificate goes stale." With the Stein RHS normalized to $-I$ (margin $\eta=1$), each edit spends $\lambda_{\max}(\Delta)$ of the budget, where $\Delta=\tilde G^{*}P\tilde E+\tilde E^{*}P\tilde G+\tilde E^{*}P\tilde E$ is Hermitian, rank $O(d)$, localized at the cut. The cached Gramian $P$ stays valid while $\eta-\sum\lambda_{\max}(\Delta)>0$; cross that and you recertify. Keep (a) and (b) separate — (a) is per-edit and spatial, (b) is cumulative and temporal.

All tiers inherit

  • Tier-1 (reach): the correction is a resolvent column sourced at the cut, decaying $\rho^{\,d(\text{cut})}$ — exactly the measured rank 2–6 far field of §4.
  • Tier-2 (residual): the bound holds on the new system, with the new conditioning risk carried solely by $S$ ($\sigma_{\min}(S)$ the new-token stiffness gate).
  • Directional / Woodbury: update $R$ by the rank-$d$ Schur term (Note #11½'s machinery).
  • Reader-set / DWR: $w_{\text{reader}}\cdot(\text{bordering source})$ is a single inner product — it certifies whether the edit changes any given reader's output with no re-solve.

Lineage to cite: bordering/Schur complement (numerical LA); prolongation–restriction + DWR (adaptive mesh refinement, DWR's native home); Fock-space / domain-perturbation (the general "change the number/shape of inputs" move).

3. The measured shadow: how far does an insert actually reach?

c2_insertdelete.py (n=8, gap-40 checkpoints). A null filler insert/delete in the filler region, diffed in the aligned frame (insert: old $i\ge c\leftrightarrow$ new $i{+}1$; delete: old $i>c\leftrightarrow$ new $i{-}1$), normalized by per-position state norm. The clean isolate is the positional-shadow ratio = insert far-field / substitution far-field on the same architecture, PE the only difference:

checkpointPEshadow ratioreading
bidir40bidir + ABS70×dense positional-reindex shadow (every shifted $h_0$ changed; downstream-dense 0.84)
bidirnp40bidir + REL2×insert ≈ substitution-at-cut — the aligned-frame reduction
curr40causal + ABS2×small but confounded (near-singular gap-40 → substitutions already reach far, ratio understates)

Relative PE cuts the insert shadow ~33× (70→2) — the aligned-frame reduction, confirmed and isolated to the PE choice. Two more facts from the same run:

  • Far-field rank 2–6 of $d{=}64$ across all checkpoints — structural-edit shadows compress to the carry "highway," so they are certifiable via a rank-$r$ update, same as substitutions.
  • Causal up_rel $\approx 0$ — the insert shadow is strictly downstream (causal structure preserved: no upstream leakage). And warm < cold at gap-40 (curr 25/53, bidir 18/34, bidirnp 38/64) — structural edits are maintainable by the aligned-frame warm start (splice a fresh slot at the cut, keep the rest).
Honest caveats. gap-40 checkpoints are near-singular, so the far field is thin (few positions past $2W$) and absolute magnitudes are large — the ratio is the clean signal, not the magnitudes; $n=8$; gap-24 is too short to have any far field (uninformative); bidirnp24 warm>cold (68/44) is the known near-singular outlier. A clean causal-relative test and a longer-context (gap $\ge 60$) substrate with a thicker far field are the owed strengtheners.

4. The negative: no cheap rigorous bordering adjoint

The tempting deployment claim: reuse the pre-edit reader adjoint, apply a Schur/Woodbury correction from cut-local blocks, and get a rigorous post-insert bound for free. colored_dwr_bordering.py tested it and it fails — cleanly, and the failure is instructive.

The Woodbury identity itself is exact: reconstruct $R_2$ to machine precision ($\sim10^{-15}$) when the support $S$ is the whole sequence. But the insert's operator change at the converged fixed point, $\Delta M = J(z_2^\star)-J(z^\star)$, is non-local:

support $S$ used$\Delta M$ mass capturedreconstruction
$\pm 8$ around cut~55% (45% outside)poor
$\pm 16$ around cut~83% (17% outside)partial
whole sequence100%$\sim 10^{-15}$ (exact)

Cause — state dependence. $J$ depends on $z$, and inserting a token shifts the equilibrium everywhere the insert reaches, at rate $\rho(G)$. So $\Delta M$ is global, not window-local: exact bordering needs $S\approx L$, and no $O(Wd)$ rigorous cheap correction exists. This refutes an earlier overclaim ("a Schur/bordering correction makes the cached cheap adjoint rigorous") — the algebra of §2 is exact for the frozen-operator response, but the operator does not stay frozen across a re-solve.

What survives — a useful partial. A warm-local bordering (correct the adjoint over a local support around the cut) does recover flip discrimination on long-range edits that the naive cached adjoint misses entirely (naive $0\times \to$ warm-Schur $13\text{–}20\times \approx$ oracle $13\text{–}29\times$), and it stays 100% sound. But it reconstructs the warm operator, not $R_2$ (reconstruction error 20–990% on large edits) — so it is a cheap approximate discriminator, not a rigorous bound. Net: cheap sound bound (naive cached adjoint) and cheap approximate long-range discrimination (warm-bordering); rigorous exact = re-solve. Structural-edit resolvent updates do not localize.

5. What it is good for: warm-start, warmer-than-warm, and RAG metering

The negative is about rigor, not utility. Two payoffs remain, both squarely in the predict→certify→correct loop (Note #11½):

  • Warm start (correcting an earlier overstatement). Insert/delete do have a warm start — the aligned-frame copy (old states at shifted positions, initialize the new slot); only the cut's $\xi$-ball re-settles. Woodbury-bordering is the "warmer-than-warm" predictor on top — so this is the same warm-vs-warmer question as value edits, not warm-vs-cold.
  • Does warmer-than-warm beat warm all-in? Conditional. The predictor is a resolvent action $R\cdot\text{source}$; a local edit touches only cached block-columns of $R$ in the $\xi$-ball $\approx$ one local iteration's work, so it pays iff it saves more than one Broyden iteration (toy: 15–30% $=$ 2–4 iters) — and only if $R$ is amortized across many edits to a fixed base. Recompute-per-edit is a wash.
The amortized regime is RAG. Insert/delete into a fixed cached base = retrieval-augmented generation, and it flips wash → net win. Mainstream RAG ([docs][query] on a decoder) is causal with a known reader, which is the clean case on three counts: (i) append is free ($b=0$ bordering, prefix preserved — "no free append on the bidirectional face" is exactly this stated in reverse); (ii) a mid-insert is one-sided (downstream causal cone; prefix untouched); (iii) the query is a known reader, so DWR applies causally despite near-singularity. Metering then has two cheap read-outs: compute cost $\propto$ chunk $\|\Delta z\|$; and the DWR answer $w_{\text{query}}\cdot(\text{chunk source})$ = "does this document change the answer" — attribution/pruning, sound and tight, with no re-solve for inert chunks. (Speculative-but-concrete: DEQ-RAG is not deployed; this is a mechanism, not a benchmark.)

6. Placement

Three claim-strengths, never mixed: the bordering algebra and the two theorems are theory (exact for the frozen-operator response); the shadow ratio (70× → 2×), the rank 2–6 far field, and the warm<cold maintainability are measured; the cheap rigorous adjoint is a measured negative (state dependence delocalizes $\Delta M$); the warm-vs-warmer net-win and RAG metering are proposed, pending the C7 maintenance run. The honest headline: an insert is a scattering center at the cut whose response reach is cheap and cut-local, but whose operator update across a re-solve is not — so reuse the geometry, price the recert, and re-solve when you need the exact answer.
