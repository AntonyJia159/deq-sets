Two Faces of the Certificate: Causality Splits the Edit-Locality Theory, and Mamba is the Linear Corner

Zhibai Jia · 3 July 2026 · the σmin story meets the causal Jacobian — one certificate becomes two, each reducing to a known linear model

What this note is. Notes #7–#8 established, on graphs, that edit-locality is governed by $\sigma_{\min}(I-J)$ rather than contraction. This note transplants that to sequences (a sliding-window softmax DEQ — an equilibrium transformer), and reports what broke and what survived when the theory met the one structural fact of the sequence setting: a causal Jacobian is block lower-triangular. That single fact splits the certificate into two faces, one of which is not Demko–Moss–Smith at all — and hands us Mamba as an exact special case.
Result, in one paragraph. The phenomenon (edit-response decays with distance; a screening length exists; warm-start local re-solve maintains it) and the invariant ($\sigma_{\min}$) survive the transplant unchanged. What restructures is the rate formula: it splits by regime into an initial-value face (causal / decode: the resolvent is a forward substitution — a linearized time-varying RNN — certified by a product–Lyapunov form, of which Mamba is the exact scalar special case) and a boundary-value face (bidirectional / edit: a screened elliptic response, certified by $\sigma_{\min}$/Faber — DMS's home turf). Experiments on the causal face confirm containment (edits decay, the envelope holds on the fair edit class) and turn up a real discovery: a causal relay must carry every binding forward, so even task-irrelevant edits propagate — the KV-cache "carry everything" behaviour emerging from the equilibrium rather than imposed on it.

1. Substrate: an equilibrium transformer on controlled-gap recall

The testbed is MQAR (multi-query associative recall): a sequence of key–value pairs, then filler, then queries that each re-name a key and must emit its bound value. The model is a sliding-window (width $w{=}10$) multi-head softmax attention cell run to equilibrium, $z=f(z)$, trained with implicit differentiation (TorchDEQ, Anderson forward + exact IFT). Two facts from the reach experiment (Note-scope C1) set up everything here: (i) the equilibrium relays recall beyond any matched finite unroll — clean separation at gap 24, where a 4-step unroll that has the mechanism (it solves gaps 0–16) still cliffs while the equilibrium holds (0.97 vs 0.36); and (ii) training pushes the cell across a range of conditioning, giving a curriculum of checkpoints spanning $\sigma_{\min}(I-J)$ from $0.18$ down to $0.03$ — the sweep the edit-locality law needs.

2. The one structural fact: a causal Jacobian is block lower-triangular

Differentiate the cell at its fixed point. The Jacobian $J$ is an $L\times L$ grid of $d\times d$ blocks, $J_{ij}=\partial f_i/\partial z_j$, and causality plus the window force $$ J_{ij}=0 \quad\text{for } j>i \ (\text{causal}) \ \text{ or } \ jbanded, block lower-triangular. Three consequences, in increasing severity:

  • Eigenvalues go blind. A triangular matrix's spectrum is the union of its diagonal blocks' spectra. So $\rho(J)$ sees only the per-token self-coupling $\partial f_i/\partial z_i$ and is completely blind to the relay — the sub-diagonal blocks that carry information forward. (This is why $\rho$ jumped between 0.72, 1.19, 8.4 across nominally-equal training runs while behaviour stayed stable: for triangular matrices, eigenvalues are fragile and uninformative.)
  • $\sigma_{\min}$ and eigenvalues decouple maximally. Triangular matrices are the textbook case where $\sigma_{\min}(I-J)$ bears no relation to $\min_i|1-\lambda_i|$. Our "$\sigma_{\min}$ governs, not $\rho$" claim is therefore structurally guaranteed in the causal setting, not an empirical surprise.
  • The old $\xi(\kappa)$ formula was a category error here. The Note-#8 conversion $\xi = -1/\ln\frac{\sqrt\kappa-1}{\sqrt\kappa+1}$ is the Demko–Moss–Smith / Chebyshev rate, proved for symmetric positive-definite banded matrices. The causal $J$ is the opposite extreme — maximally non-normal — so that formula was never justified for it. (It was defensible on the graphs, whose undirected topology makes $J$ nearer-normal; that near-normality is now a flagged check, not an assumption.)
Honest correction to the framework. The certificate quantity ($\sigma_{\min}$, and its roles: invertibility, gain $1/\sigma_{\min}$, error amplification) was right all along and needs no symmetry. What was wrong was one step: converting conditioning into a spatial decay rate via the SPD Chebyshev formula. That step is what splits below into two regime-specific replacements.

4. The product–Lyapunov certificate (causal face)

Split $J=D+N$ (block-diagonal self-coupling vs strictly-lower coupling band). Then $$ (I-J)^{-1}=\sum_{k=0}^{L-1}\big[(I-D)^{-1}N\big]^k\,(I-D)^{-1}, $$ and because $N$ is strictly lower-triangular, $(I-D)^{-1}N$ is nilpotent — the sum terminates. This is an exact identity requiring no convergence condition: it holds even at the $\rho(J)=8.4$ checkpoint where the naive Neumann series $\sum J^k$ diverges. The influence of an edit at $j$ on position $i$ is then a finite sum of ordered products of the coupling blocks along forward paths, with dwells folded into the $(I-D_i)^{-1}$ factors. The resulting screening rate is $$ g \;=\; \max_i \big\|(I-D_i)^{-1}[\,W_i\cdots A_i\,]\big\| \;\le\; \frac{\max_i\|[\,W_i\cdots A_i\,]\|}{1-\max_i\|D_i\|}, $$ — coupling per hop $\times$ token-internal re-amplification $1/(1-\|D\|)$. The per-token $\|(I-D_i)^{-1}\|=1/\sigma_{\min}(I-D_i)$: the global certificate factorizes into per-token $\sigma_{\min}$ times coupling norms — the same invariant, one block at a time. Cost is $O(L\,w\,d^2)$: linear in sequence length, no inverse, no eigensolve — the only one of the three certificates that scales, with $\sigma_{\min}$/FOV as the $O((Ld)^3)$ toy-scale validation layer.

Exactness is a property of the scalar single-path chain; with dense $d\times d$ blocks and $w$ interfering paths the product form becomes a sound upper bound, whose slack we validate against the exact resolvent.

5. Mamba is the linear corner — the DEQ properties, visible in a shipped model

Mamba is not a baseline we race; it is the exact scalar special case of the causal certificate, and therefore the cleanest concrete anchor for every quantity in this note. Its selective SSM $h_t=\bar A_t h_{t-1}+\bar B_t x_t$ has a state-sequence Jacobian that is block lower-bidiagonal with diagonal coupling blocks $\bar A_t$ and no self-coupling. So:

  • The product form is an identity, not a bound. Influence of time $j$ on time $i$ is exactly $\bar A_i\cdots\bar A_{j+1}=\prod_t\lambda_t$ — one path, verified numerically above. Our causal certificate is this with dense blocks and multiple paths.
  • Selectivity is a Lyapunov schedule. The input-dependent gate sets $\lambda_t\!\approx\!1$ to carry, $\lambda_t\!\ll\!1$ to forget — per-channel, per-step control of the influence product. One near-zero gate severs the chain: Mamba's memory management is the product-form certificate, actively steered.
  • Memory horizon is edit reach. The literature tunes $\lambda\!\to\!1$ (horizon $1/(1-\rho)$) to remember; read backward the identical number is the screening length of a perturbation. Long memory $\equiv$ far-reaching edits. Mamba performs the forward reading; the maintenance reading was never taken, for a structural reason — an SSM's fused state has no mid-context edit operation to price.
  • It is a linear equilibrium. S4/static is the constant-coefficient case $\lambda_t\equiv\lambda$: a linear fixed point whose resolvent decay is the $1/(1-\lambda)$ we have been calling the $\rho$ story. The DEQ properties (fixed point, resolvent, screening length) are already present in it — latent, because linearity collapses the general $\sigma_{\min}$ down to $1-\rho$.
How to cite it (positioning). Mamba appears as a recurring linear anchor — a worked example that grounds each abstract object (product certificate, the $\rho{\to}1$ duality, the detach-from-linearity thesis) in a shipped model — not as a section and not as a benchmark rival. The generalization ladder is exact at every rung: scalar (Mamba channels) $\to$ diagonal blocks (Mamba layer) $\to$ dense $d\times d$ blocks, width $w$, multi-path (ours). Our carry can be a low-dimensional subspace precisely because our blocks mix features, which Mamba's diagonal blocks cannot. (A re-blocking argument — group $w$ positions — makes any causal band block bidiagonal at window granularity, so at the coarse level our certificate is literally Mamba-shaped, wide blocks in place of scalars. "One hop $=$ one window" is then a theorem, not a unit convention.)

6. Experiments: the causal face measured

We measure edit-locality directly: solve a sequence, substitute one token, re-solve from the old equilibrium (the maintenance operation), and read the response $|\Delta z_i|$ vs distance. The measurement took four revisions to get right, and each correction was the object being more structured than the hypothesis.

6a. Edits have task-relevance classes — and a causal relay must carry every binding

The first naive single-exponential fits "violated" the envelope. The cause was not the model: a task-relevant edit (a queried value) must reach its dependent query — that is recall working, a transport ridge, not a locality failure. Splitting edits by relevance revealed a genuine discovery. A causal relay cannot know which keys will be queried later, so it transports every binding forward; thus even a task-irrelevant value edit rides the carry. On filler edits, binned at hop ($=$ window) granularity — the resolution the re-blocking argument says is the right one — the measured reach sits inside the (loose, wrong-face) Faber envelope everywhere it is measurable, and grows as $\sigma_{\min}$ shrinks (the conditioning-governs-reach trend, on the witness class):

6c. $\sigma_{\min}$ has a third face: error amplification

Anderson stalled at residual $\sim\!10^{-4}$ regardless of budget on these peaked cells, and two nominally "converged" states sat up to $10^{-2}$ apart. This is not multistability; it is $\|z_{\text{err}}\|\le \text{resid}/\sigma_{\min}$ — the same conditioning that sets reach and solve cost also amplifies solver error. Confirmed directly: a 2–3 step Newton polish $z\!\mathrel{+}=(I-J)^{-1}(f(z)-z)$ (fp32 evaluation, fp64 solve) tightens the residual $10^{-4}\!\to\!10^{-8}$, and the warm-vs-cold discrepancy collapses right along with it. So $\sigma_{\min}$ now appears four ways — reach, solve cost, error amplification, and the per-token factorization of §4.

6d. Warm-start is exact under uniqueness, branch-tracking otherwise

With tight residuals, warm and cold re-solves agree to $10^{-7}$ wherever the fixed point is unique (curr00–24: per-sequence cold-vs-cold divergence $\sim\!10^{-7}$). Two distinct multistability signals appear: curr40 is sequence-dependent (2 of 4 base sequences admit multiple equilibria; the half-trained $\rho=8.4$ cell), while curr16 shows an edit-induced split (unique before the edit, branched after). Where uniqueness fails, warm-start is not "approximately exact" — it is branch-tracking: it follows the equilibrium continuous with the pre-edit state, which is arguably the correct maintenance semantics (a cold recompute may jump branches). The $\sigma_{\min}$ uniqueness certificate is exactly the condition under which "cheap" and "correct" coincide — and it connects the maintenance story to beneficial solution-diversity (a separately-flagged direction).

7. What survived, what restructured, what is owed

The restructure, weighed. Three layers: the phenomenon (decay, screening length, warm-start maintenance) is untouched; the invariant $\sigma_{\min}$ is untouched and strengthened (now four appearances); only the rate formula is rebuilt — into two faces that each reduce exactly to a known linear model (Chebyshev $\leftrightarrow$ graph resolvent; product $\leftrightarrow$ Mamba). The "generalize the linear incumbents" thesis now holds on both faces, provably, rather than via one stretched formula. This is a stronger theory section, not damage control.
  • The bidirectional face — our actual use case — is not yet measured. Editing is the boundary-value regime, where Faber/$\sigma_{\min}$ is proper; but we measured the causal checkpoints because they existed. A bidirectional-band curriculum retrain is the headline experiment still owed.
  • The product certificate is not yet computed on real $J$. Only the $5\times5$ toy and the exact resolvent exist; the block-form $g$ (and the sharper geometric-mean-along-path rate) must be run on the trained Jacobians and scored against the exact resolvent to measure its slack.
  • A literature debt in a second field. The causal face is exponential-dichotomy / Lyapunov theory; whether sparse-attention or RNN-Jacobian locality has already been read through that lens (the Mamba-as-product observation feels findable) is unchecked.
  • FOV can be vacuous. For strongly non-normal $J$ the field of values can contain $0$ while $J$ is invertible — the Faber bound then abstains. This is a feature (fails safe) but must be stated, and it is why the causal regime needs the product form instead.
  • curr40 is half-trained ($\rho=8.4$, recall $0.83$); its multistability and loose residuals are consistent with an unconverged cell, and it is used only as the extreme conditioning point, not as a clean equilibrium.
